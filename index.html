About Me

I am a PhD candidate at Tel Aviv University, advised by Oren Barkan. My research is at the intersection of Deep Learning, Computer Vision, and Explainable AI (XAI). I focus on developing methods that make complex black-box models more interpretable and aligned with human cognition.

My work includes developing novel attribution methods like Iterated Integrated Attributions and Stochastic Integrated Explanations, which have been applied across vision models, recommender systems, and large language models (LLMs).

I am a PhD Candidate nearing the completion of my doctoral studies, advised by Oren Barkan. My research focuses on Continual Learning (CL) for computer vision and Explainable AI (XAI). I am dedicated to making deep learning models more transparent and adaptive through methods such as saliency maps, Layer-wise Relevance Propagation (LRP), and concept-based interpretability.

Prior to my research career, I gained extensive industry experience as a Software Engineer, building large-scale Java REST APIs and big data applications. Currently, I am also the manager of a vibrant research community, bridging the gap between theoretical AI and practical implementation.
